Trash Collection Guide

Trash Prep & Recycling Service Tips

TRASH BASICS
  • All trash must be tied and bagged - no loose items
  • Double-bag anything leaky (food, diapers, pet waste)
  • Got extra bags? Stack them “snow-cone style” inside your bin
RECYCLING RULES THAT MATTER
  • We do single-stream recycling: paper, plastic, metal, and glass can mix
  • Rinse containers and keep caps on
  • Cardboard must be broken down flat
  • If you're unsure... toss it in trash to avoid contamination
KEEP OUT OF BINS
  • Furniture, bulk items, sharp objects
  • Electronics, batteries, or hazardous waste
  • Paint, chemicals, or flammable liquids
  • Medical waste or prescription drugs
  • Construction debris like bricks, concrete, or drywall

Let’s work together to keep the good stuff recyclable and the trash tidy. Well-sorted bins make for happy communities.

The Do’s and Don’ts of Your Bin

Trash
Recycling
Compost

ACCEPTABLE TRASH ITEMS:

  • Food waste (bagged)
  • Diapers, animal waste (double-bagged)
  • Plastic wrap, containers, and bags
  • Cans, bottles, cartons, styrofoam

NON-ACCEPTABLE TRASH ITEMS:

  • Electronics, medical waste, furniture
  • Paint, large appliances, broken glass

ACCEPTABLE RECYCLING:

  • Hard plastics (milk jugs, detergent bottles)
  • Cardboard boxes (broken down)
  • Clean paper, magazines, envelopes
  • Glass bottles and jars
  • Aluminum and metal cans

DO NOT RECYCLE:

  • Plastic bags, styrofoam, food-soiled paper
  • Electronics, wax paper, liquids

Composting Tips (Optional for Participating Properties)

  • Use paper or BPI-certified compost bags
  • Compostable: food scraps, pizza boxes, napkins, flowers
  • Do NOT compost: glass, plastic, liquids, metal

Composting’s a team sport and you’re crushing it.
